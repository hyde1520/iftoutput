How to Repair Uploaded Shapefiles

When uploading shapefiles with multiple adjoining polygons, IFTDSS will automatically join the polygons into a single dataset; this process may create erroneous gaps or vertices where these polygons once touched. The steps below outline the process of repairing or cleaning these polygons and re-saving them as a new shapefile.

More Functionality to Come! These occasional gaps in adjoining polygons are a known issue which we will be resolving in a forthcoming version of IFTDSS.

Step 1 Viewing your shapefile in Map Studio

Once you have uploaded and saved your shapefile, navigate to the Layer List, a checked box to the left of the shapefile confirms your layer is operational. If this box is not checked, click on the box to select it. If your shapefile is not visible in the Layer List, you may have to add it to your map. Zoom to your shapefile by selecting the Zoom To option under More Options in the top right of the Layer List .

Step 2 Enable Editing

Open Edit Tools from the bottom of Map Studio and

Select Copy from Existing Features . To only edit one polygon choose Select Singlepart, or edit multiple polygons choose Select multipart.

Scroll to the map and double left click the mouse button over the polygon you intend to edit, this will reveal the polygon vertices and an editing box

Step 3 Edit your shapefile

Move a vertex by hovering over it, holding down the left mouse key, and dragging it to the correct position.

Delete a vertex by hovering over it, clicking the right mouse button once, and selecting delete

Continue your edits until you are satisfied with your shapefile.

Step 4 Save your Edits

When you are finished, save the corrections as a new shapefile by giving it a descriptive name in Edit Tools, specify the folder to save the file to, and select Create. The new shapefile will now be available in MyWorkspace for use throughout IFTDSS. NOTE THIS FEATURE IS NOT WORKING CURRENTLY 2-28-17-JH
